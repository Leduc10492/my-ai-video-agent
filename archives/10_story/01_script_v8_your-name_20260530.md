FADE IN:

EXT. TOKYO - DAWN

First trains cut through a pale city.

Twenty-five-year-old REN ASAKURA wakes against a subway window.

His cheek is wet.

He wipes it, looks at his palm.

No mark.

Across the platform, twenty-eight-year-old YUNA HOSHINO steps from another train.

She also wipes tears.

Neither sees the other.

The trains move in opposite directions.

EXT. SKY OVER JAPAN - NIGHT - YEARS EARLIER

A comet crosses the sky.

One bright piece separates from the tail.

It falls behind clouds.

TITLE: 《无名之绳》

INT. REN'S APARTMENT - TOKYO - MORNING

Seventeen-year-old REN wakes in a room full of model buildings.

He stares at his hands.

His younger sister AKI bangs on the door.

AKI (O.S.)
Ren, homeroom.

Ren opens his notebook.

On one page, in handwriting that is not his:

WHO ARE YOU?

He flips pages.

A second note:

YOUR TRAINS ARE A TRAP.

Ren looks at his phone.

Three photos: a lake, a shrine rope, his own face reflected in a vending machine.

INT. YUNA'S ROOM - MOUNTAIN VILLAGE - MORNING

Seventeen-year-old YUNA wakes on a futon.

Her grandmother ASA opens the sliding door.

ASA
Shrine duty.

Yuna touches her short hair.

She looks down at the shrine robe folded beside her.

YUNA
Why am I back?

Her little brother SORA pokes his head in.

SORA
You asked where the men's train pass was.

Yuna freezes.

EXT. NAGI VILLAGE - MORNING

Yuna runs down stone steps past rice fields, election posters, and red cord streamers.

The village, NAGI, sits beside a round lake.

Above the road, a comet poster reads:

WHITE BROOM COMET FESTIVAL - ONE MONTH.

An election truck rolls by.

Yuna's father, MAYOR HOSHINO, speaks from loudspeakers.

MAYOR (V.O.)
Tradition protects Nagi.

Yuna ducks behind a wall.

INT. TOKYO HIGH SCHOOL - DAY

Ren enters class late.

His friends KENTA and MIO stare.

KENTA
You bowed to the vending machine.

REN
I did not.

MIO
You called the escalator a moving cliff.

Ren sits.

His notebook has a village map drawn across the math page.

Teacher KUBOTA stops beside him.

KUBOTA
If urban planning is more important than algebra, teach the next problem.

Ren stands.

He does not know the problem.

On his palm, in tiny handwriting:

ANSWER: 12.

REN
Twelve.

The class laughs.

Kubota checks.

He frowns.

INT. NAGI HIGH SCHOOL - DAY

Yuna sits in class.

Her friends HANA and DAICHI lean close.

HANA
Today you're normal.

YUNA
Yesterday?

DAICHI
You kicked the door open and asked where Tokyo keeps the sky.

The class laughs.

Teacher NAKAI points to Yuna.

NAKAI
Since you're energetic, read.

Yuna stands.

Her textbook is full of blocky handwriting:

SORRY. DID NOT KNOW THIS WAS YOUR BODY.

Yuna shuts the book.

YUNA
I forgot my voice.

Silence.

Hana coughs to hide a laugh.

EXT. NAGI SCHOOL FIELD - LUNCH

Hana, Daichi, and Yuna sit near the track.

Hana holds Yuna's phone.

HANA
You don't remember sending this?

Video plays: Ren in Yuna's body sprinting at the vending machine, pressing every button, then saluting the hot coffee can.

Daichi wheezes.

Yuna grabs the phone.

YUNA
Delete.

DAICHI
This is town history.

HANA
No. This is evidence for blackmail.

Yuna looks at the lake beyond the fence.

YUNA
What if it wasn't a dream?

The other two stop laughing.

INT. REN'S APARTMENT - NIGHT

Ren writes in his phone:

RULE 1: LEAVE NOTES.
RULE 2: NO SPENDING MONEY.
RULE 3: NO CONFESSIONS.
RULE 4: DO NOT RUIN MY JOB.

He hesitates.

Adds:

RULE 5: IF YOU WAKE UP SCARED, WRITE FIRST.

INT. YUNA'S ROOM - NIGHT

Yuna reads the rules.

She writes below:

RULE 6: LEARN HOW TO BOW LIKE A HUMAN.

She pauses.

Adds:

RULE 7: DO NOT MAKE MY FRIENDS WORRY.

INT. ITALIAN RESTAURANT - TOKYO - NIGHT

Yuna, inside Ren's body, carries plates through a narrow restaurant.

She moves carefully, too carefully.

Chef MARUI points to table seven.

MARUI
Pasta dies in ten seconds.

Yuna sprints.

She delivers the plate.

Her senior coworker RIKA catches the falling pepper mill.

RIKA
You smiled today.

Yuna, as Ren, bows too deeply.

RIKA
And now you are apologizing to pepper.

YUNA/REN
Pepper works hard.

Rika laughs.

Across the room, Ren's manager watches.

MANAGER
Asakura. Less theater. More water.

Yuna grabs a pitcher, bumps a chair, recovers, and serves water without spilling.

Rika raises an eyebrow.

RIKA
Better.

INT. NAGI SHRINE STAGE - NIGHT

Ren, inside Yuna's body, stands in shrine clothing before villagers.

Drums begin.

Hana whispers from the side.

HANA
Left hand first.

Ren raises the wrong hand.

The crowd murmurs.

Asa taps a drum once.

Ren watches Asa's fingers.

He follows.

The dance steadies.

On the final turn, he sees the comet hanging over the lake.

After the dance, Mayor Hoshino walks past without applause.

MAYOR
At least your mistake looked old.

Ren, as Yuna, turns.

YUNA/REN
Is that a compliment from a father or a mayor?

The aides freeze.

Mayor Hoshino looks at his daughter with new suspicion.

INT. MIYAMORI SHRINE - STOREHOUSE - LATE NIGHT

Asa teaches Ren, in Yuna's body, to braid red ceremonial cord.

ASA
A cord is time you can hold.

Ren pulls too hard.

The cord knots.

ASA
Do not fight the thread.

REN/YUNA
What happens if it breaks?

ASA
Then you learn whether you tied a knot or made a promise.

Asa takes an old sealed gourd from a cabinet.

ASA
Tomorrow, we carry this to the crater shrine.

Ren watches the family crest on the gourd.

INT. REN'S ROOM - MORNING

Ren wakes.

He checks his phone.

Yuna has written:

YOUR JOB IS CHAOS. YOUR SENIOR IS BEAUTIFUL.

Ren shouts.

REN
No.

He scrolls.

There is a date marked on his calendar.

This Saturday.

Under it:

YOU OWE ME A CITY DAY.

INT. YUNA'S ROOM - MORNING

Yuna wakes.

She sees Ren's note:

YOUR GRANDMOTHER KNOWS MORE THAN SHE SAYS.

Yuna looks toward the shrine courtyard.

Asa sweeps leaves below.

INT. TOKYO HIGH SCHOOL - DAY

Yuna, inside Ren's body, sits in art class.

The class sketches a plaster hand.

Yuna draws the hand wrapped in a red cord.

Mio looks over.

MIO
Since when do you draw like you miss someone?

Yuna stops.

The teacher places Ren's old sketch beside it.

The old sketch is all straight lines.

The new one is all knots.

INT. NAGI TOWN OFFICE - DAY

Ren, inside Yuna's body, waits outside the mayor's office with a document tray.

An aide hands him campaign flyers.

AIDE
Smile and pass these at the festival meeting.

Ren reads the flyer.

It promises safe viewing zones by the lake.

He looks out the window at the crater shrine ridge.

REN/YUNA
Who checks the evacuation routes?

The aide laughs.

AIDE
For a comet party?

Ren folds one flyer into his pocket.

EXT. TOKYO SIDE STREET - NIGHT

Yuna, inside Ren's body, runs after a departing bus.

She misses it.

Rika catches up, carrying Ren's forgotten sketch tube.

RIKA
You remember impossible streets, but not bus times.

Yuna takes the tube.

YUNA/REN
In my town, the bus waits because everyone knows the driver.

Rika studies him.

RIKA
That sounds lonely in the opposite direction.

Yuna has no joke ready.

INT. YUNA'S ROOM - NIGHT

Yuna, herself, finds a new note from Ren:

YOUR FATHER'S OFFICE HAS NO EVACUATION MAP.

Yuna opens her closet.

Behind school uniforms, she pulls out old town disaster pamphlets.

She lays them beside Ren's Tokyo train map.

The two maps do not fit.

YUNA
Then we make one that does.

EXT. NAGI RIDGE - DAY

Yuna, herself, walks with Asa and Sora toward an old crater shrine above the lake.

Sora carries a box of braided cords.

ASA
The old shrine is across the waterline.

YUNA
Why keep offerings where no one visits?

ASA
Because some promises are made to days, not people.

They cross a rope bridge.

Sora looks down and freezes.

Yuna takes his hand.

YUNA
Count my steps.

SORA
You hate counting.

YUNA
Today I don't.

INT. CRATER SHRINE - DAY

Hundreds of red cords hang from the ceiling.

Asa places the sealed gourd on stone.

ASA
This holds rice water, breath, and the name of the maker.

Yuna touches an old cord.

For one second, she hears a Tokyo train.

She pulls back.

ASA
What did you hear?

YUNA
A door closing.

Asa does not look surprised.

EXT. TOKYO - WEEKEND - DAY

Ren, himself, waits outside a gallery.

Rika arrives.

REN
Thank you for coming.

RIKA
You sound like someone proofread you.

Ren touches the red cord tied around his wrist.

He does not remember tying it.

INT. GALLERY - DAY

Ren and Rika walk through architectural models.

Ren talks too fast.

Rika stops at a photograph of a mountain lake.

RIKA
You drew this place, didn't you?

Ren sees the photo label:

NAGI BASIN, ARCHIVE IMAGE.

He steps closer.

The lake is the village from his dreams.

INT. GALLERY CAFE - LATER

Ren and Rika sit with untouched coffee.

Rika slides a napkin toward him.

On it, she has written three words:

PERSON.
PLACE.
TIME.

RIKA
Pick one. That is what you are actually searching for.

Ren writes under PERSON:

I DON'T KNOW.

Under PLACE:

NAGI.

Under TIME:

He stops.

The red cord tightens against his wrist.

RIKA
That's the one.

Ren folds the napkin.

REN
I am sorry.

RIKA
Do not apologize for being late to your own life.

EXT. TOKYO PEDESTRIAN BRIDGE - SUNSET

Rika hands Ren a bandage.

RIKA
You're looking for someone.

REN
Maybe.

RIKA
Then don't invite someone else on the search.

She leaves.

Ren looks at the red cord.

INT. REN'S ROOM - NIGHT

Ren calls Yuna.

No signal.

He sends messages.

Nothing.

He sleeps with his phone in his hand.

Morning.

Still himself.

Another morning.

Still himself.

He opens every note they wrote.

Yuna's messages begin disappearing.

He copies one sentence onto paper before it vanishes:

THE LAKE IS A BELL.

INT. NAGI HIGH SCHOOL - DAY

Yuna, herself, stares at her phone.

Ren's notes are fading.

Hana sits beside her.

HANA
Did Tokyo boy dump you?

Yuna almost laughs.

YUNA
I don't know if he existed.

Daichi drops a soda can on the desk.

DAICHI
Then why did I dream I owed him money?

Yuna opens the phone.

One note remains:

IF THE COMET SPLITS, RUN.

The text fades.

Hana reads the last word before it vanishes.

HANA
Run from what?

Yuna looks out at the comet festival banners.

EXT. RURAL TRAIN - DAY

Ren, Kenta, and Mio travel into the mountains.

Ren holds sketches of Nagi's streets.

Mio has printed old maps.

Kenta carries snacks like emergency gear.

MIO
Your dream town has no current address.

REN
Then it had an old one.

KENTA
That sentence should worry us.

EXT. SMALL STATION - LATE AFTERNOON

They show Ren's sketch to a station attendant.

ATTENDANT
Not here.

He pauses.

ATTENDANT
But my father used to say there was a village with a round lake.

Ren steps forward.

The attendant points down the road.

ATTENDANT
Ask the noodle shop before the owner starts drinking.

INT. NOODLE SHOP - EVENING

The cook studies Ren's drawing.

COOK
Who gave you this?

REN
No one.

COOK
Then you stole a ghost.

He points to a framed newspaper.

The headline shows a comet impact.

Ren stands.

The room sound drops away.

Kenta reads the date.

KENTA
Three years ago.

Mio looks at Ren.

MIO
You were in Tokyo.

Ren cannot answer.

EXT. NAGI RUINS OVERLOOK - NIGHT

Ren looks over a massive lake.

No village.

Only water and memorial lights.

Mio checks her phone.

MIO
Impact zone. Evacuation incomplete. Casualties...

She stops.

Ren shakes his head.

REN
I was there last week.

Kenta touches his shoulder.

Ren pulls away.

INT. LOCAL LIBRARY - NIGHT

Ren scrolls through disaster records.

Faces of the dead.

He finds Hana.

He finds Daichi.

He finds Sora.

He finds Asa listed as missing, not dead.

He finds no Yuna.

For one second he breathes.

Then Mio turns a page.

MIO
Family registry update.

Yuna's photo appears in a memorial booklet.

Ren grips the table.

The librarian turns off one row of lights.

LIBRARIAN
We're closing.

Ren writes YUNA HOSHINO on his palm.

The ink fades before it dries.

EXT. MEMORIAL BUS STOP - NIGHT

Kenta opens an umbrella over Ren.

KENTA
Come back with us.

REN
I need the shrine.

MIO
The crater trail is closed.

REN
Then I need the closed trail.

Mio grabs his sleeve.

MIO
If this is grief, we can help. If this is real, we still can.

Ren stops.

He looks at both of them.

REN
Find every evacuation report. Anything with the school, the mayor, the broadcast system.

Kenta nods.

KENTA
That's a terrible plan.

MIO
But finally a plan.

EXT. MOUNTAIN TRAIL - DAWN

Ren climbs alone toward the crater shrine.

Rain begins.

He slips.

The red cord on his wrist catches on a branch.

He sees the shrine cave across the waterline.

INT. CRATER SHRINE - DAY

The hanging cords sway though there is no wind.

Ren finds the sealed gourd.

It bears Yuna's family crest.

He opens it.

Inside: fermented festival water and a braided cord.

Ren kneels.

REN
If this is a door, open.

He drinks.

The cave tilts.

The cords pull upward like falling rain in reverse.

CUT TO BLACK.

INT. YUNA'S ROOM - MORNING

Ren wakes in Yuna's body.

Sora opens the door.

SORA
Festival day.

Ren runs to the mirror.

Yuna's face stares back.

He laughs once.

Then remembers the memorial booklet.

REN/YUNA
Today.

EXT. NAGI STREET - DAY

Ren, as Yuna, runs through town.

Children hang comet lanterns.

Election trucks pass.

Mayor Hoshino speaks from a platform.

MAYOR (V.O.)
Tonight, Nagi welcomes the comet.

Ren pushes through the crowd.

REN/YUNA
You have to evacuate.

The mayor sees his daughter's face.

MAYOR
Not here.

INT. MAYOR'S OFFICE - DAY

Ren stands across from the mayor.

The mayor's aides hover.

REN/YUNA
The fragment hits after sunset. The lake becomes a crater.

MAYOR
Who put you up to this?

REN/YUNA
No one.

MAYOR
Then stop speaking like a stranger in my daughter's mouth.

Ren freezes.

The mayor knows something.

MAYOR
Your mother spoke that way before she died.

MAYOR
Go home.

HANA
You're pale.

REN/YUNA

DAICHI
Again?

REN/YUNA
We need everyone at the school before dark.

Hana looks at the festival stalls.

HANA
That's not a prank.

REN/YUNA
No.

DAICHI

INT. SCHOOL BROADCAST ROOM - AFTERNOON

HANA
What if they arrest us?

REN/YUNA
They can arrest us at the school.

Daichi looks at him.

DAICHI
You talk like you won't be here tomorrow.

EXT. SCHOOL COURTYARD - AFTERNOON

Yuna, in her own body again, opens Ren's evacuation map.

The ink is fading.

Hana holds one corner.

Daichi holds the other.

HANA
Why is his handwriting disappearing?

Because morning is trying to win.

DAICHI
If we get people there by seven-ten, the hill blocks the blast.

HANA
It is five-forty.

7:10 SCHOOL.
7:35 IMPACT.

INT. HANA'S HOUSE - AFTERNOON

Hana packs bandages into a school bag.

HANA'S MOTHER
Festival shift starts in ten minutes.

HANA
I need to help Yuna.

HANA'S MOTHER
Yuna is mayor trouble. You are not.

Hana takes the family emergency radio from the shelf.

HANA
Tonight everyone is mayor trouble.

EXT. SUBSTATION - SUNSET

Daichi sets a timed smoke charge.

DAICHI
My dad built this.

REN/YUNA
Can you make it fail without hurting anyone?

Daichi nods.

Hana watches the comet brighten.

HANA
It is too pretty to be a warning.

Daichi hits it.

Nothing.

He opens the casing.

DAICHI
I need two minutes.

REN/YUNA
You have one.

Nothing.

DAICHI
It will smoke, not trip.

HANA
Six-ten.

Ren looks toward town.

REN/YUNA

DAICHI
My dad is going to kill me.

EXT. CRATER SHRINE RIDGE - SUNSET

Silence.

You're shorter than your notes.

REN
You're worse at apologies than your notes.

REN
Write your name.

REN
You first.

Yuna writes on Ren's.

YUNA
If I forget?

Run anyway.

The ink is smeared.

Only one stroke remains.

EXT. NAGI FESTIVAL - NIGHT

YUNA
Broadcast now.

Forward.

INT. SCHOOL BROADCAST ROOM - NIGHT

Daichi slams the mixer.

DAICHI
Substation did not trip.

Yuna looks toward town hall.

YUNA
Then we use his voice.

EXT. SCHOOL GYM - NIGHT

TEACHER

HANA
Then authorize catching.

The teacher lowers the clipboard.

TEACHER
North wall. Keep them away from glass.

Hana starts moving children.

EXT. NAGI BRIDGE - NIGHT

Daichi drives a farm cart loaded with elders.

The bridge is jammed with festival cars.

He honks.

No movement.

He looks at the clock on the cart dashboard.

7:18.

He jumps down and starts folding side mirrors by hand.

DAICHI
If you love your car, move it uphill!

EXT. NAGI STREET - NIGHT

Hana runs with the emergency radio.

Daichi cuts through festival stalls toward the backup generator.

People laugh at the first warning.

A vendor keeps grilling.

An old man refuses to leave his stall.

Hana grabs his cash box and runs uphill.

OLD MAN
Thief!

HANA
Yes! Follow me!

He follows.

INT. TOWN HALL - NIGHT

The mayor listens to aides argue.

Yuna bursts in.

YUNA
Mom knew, didn't she?

The room goes quiet.

MAYOR
Leave.

YUNA
She left cords for days she could not reach.

The mayor's mask cracks.

YUNA
You heard her once and chose being mayor instead of being believed.

The mayor raises his hand.

He cannot strike her.

Outside, the first siren begins.

The mayor looks at the comet on the security monitor.

He picks up the emergency phone.

MAYOR
Open the school shelter.

EXT. NAGI - NIGHT

Town speakers come alive.

People look up from festival stalls.

Children carry lanterns toward the school.

Daichi gets the backup generator coughing.

Hana drags the old man through the school gate.

Yuna stands in the street.

The comet fragment tears open the sky.

EXT. SCHOOL HILL - NIGHT

INT. TOKYO APARTMENT - MORNING

Ren wakes.

He gasps.

His palm is blank.

He grabs paper.

He writes:

Y...

The rest will not come.

On his wrist is the red cord.

EXT. NAGI MEMORIAL PARK - YEARS LATER - DAY

Ren, twenty-five, stands before a new town map.

The old crater lake is marked as preserved water.

A plaque lists evacuation survivors.

He reads names.

He does not know what he is looking for.

Yuna's name is there as a planning volunteer.

He traces it.

The letters mean nothing.

INT. TEMPORARY HOUSING ARCHIVE - DAY

Yuna, twenty-eight, files old evacuation maps into archival sleeves.

A young planner sets a box beside her.

PLANNER
More Nagi interviews.

Yuna opens one folder.

Inside is a child's drawing of a school gym full of lanterns.

At the corner, someone drew a boy and a girl holding a red line.

Yuna touches the line.

She closes the folder before the planner sees.

INT. TOKYO ARCHITECTURE OFFICE - DAY

Ren works on a community shelter model.

His coworker places coffee beside him.

COWORKER
You design every roof like it is waiting for rain.

REN
Maybe every roof is.

EXT. TOKYO STREET - WINTER EVENING

Yuna, twenty-eight, exits a planning office with rolled maps.

She wears a red cord around her bag handle.

She stops before a poster:

REGIONAL DISASTER MEMORY EXHIBITION.

Neither turns.

The cord swings.

INT. PLANNING EXHIBITION - NIGHT

Yuna studies Ren's shelter model.

Ren enters behind her, late for his own presentation.

Yuna turns as he bends to pick up a fallen label.
