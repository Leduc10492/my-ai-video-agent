# 《无名之绳》v1

## Scope

This is a Screenwriter workflow draft built from publicly available high-level story information about `Your Name.` and then prepared for comparison against the user-provided PDF. It is intentionally a functional draft rather than a transcript or remake. Names, place names, dialogue, ritual design, and many scene expressions are original for this project exercise.

## Screenplay

```text
FADE IN:

EXT. TOKYO - DAWN

First trains cut through a pale city.

Twenty-five-year-old REN ASAKURA wakes against a subway window.

His cheek is wet.

He wipes it, looks at his palm.

No mark.

Across the platform, twenty-eight-year-old YUNA HOSHINO steps from another train.

She also wipes tears.

Neither sees the other.

CUT TO:

EXT. SKY OVER JAPAN - NIGHT - YEARS EARLIER

A comet crosses the sky.

One bright piece separates from the tail.

It falls behind clouds.

TITLE: 《无名之绳》

INT. REN'S APARTMENT - TOKYO - MORNING

Seventeen-year-old REN wakes in a room full of model buildings.

He stares at his hands.

His younger sister bangs on the door.

SISTER (O.S.)
Ren, you'll miss homeroom.

Ren opens his notebook.

On one page, in handwriting that is not his:

WHO ARE YOU?

INT. YUNA'S ROOM - MOUNTAIN VILLAGE - MORNING

Seventeen-year-old YUNA wakes on a futon.

Her grandmother, ASA, opens the sliding door.

ASA
Yuna. Shrine duty.

Yuna sits up.

She touches her short hair.

She looks down at the shrine robe folded beside her.

YUNA
Why am I back?

Her little brother SORA pokes his head in.

SORA
You said yesterday you were a Tokyo boy.

Yuna freezes.

EXT. MOUNTAIN VILLAGE - MORNING

Yuna runs down stone steps past rice fields, election posters, and red cord streamers.

The village, NAGI, sits beside a round lake.

Above the lake, a comet poster reads:

WHITE BROOM COMET FESTIVAL - ONE MONTH.

INT. TOKYO HIGH SCHOOL - DAY

Ren moves through the hallway.

His friends, KENTA and MIO, stare at him.

KENTA
You bowed to the vending machine.

REN
I did not.

MIO
You also called me "city person."

Ren opens his phone.

There are photos of village rooftops, a shrine courtyard, and his own face in a convenience-store reflection.

INT. NAGI HIGH SCHOOL - DAY

Yuna sits in class.

Her friends, HANA and DAICHI, lean close.

HANA
Today you're normal.

YUNA
Yesterday?

DAICHI
You kicked the door open and asked where the train station was.

The class laughs.

Yuna looks at her notebook.

Someone has drawn a Tokyo street map.

Under it:

STOP TOUCHING MY LIFE.

INT. MIYAMORI SHRINE - STOREHOUSE - EVENING

Asa teaches Yuna to braid red ceremonial cord.

ASA
A cord remembers shape after the hand leaves it.

Yuna pulls too hard.

The cord knots.

ASA
Do not fight the thread.

Yuna looks at the tangled red cord.

CUT TO:

INT. REN'S APARTMENT - NIGHT

Ren writes in his phone:

RULE 1: LEAVE NOTES.
RULE 2: NO SPENDING MONEY.
RULE 3: NO CONFESSIONS.

He stops.

Adds:

RULE 4: DO NOT RUIN MY JOB.

INT. ITALIAN RESTAURANT - TOKYO - NIGHT

Yuna, inside Ren's body, carries plates through a narrow restaurant.

She moves carefully, too carefully.

Chef MARUI points to table seven.

MARUI
Pasta dies in ten seconds.

Yuna sprints.

She delivers the plate.

Her senior coworker RIKA catches the falling pepper mill.

RIKA
You smiled today.

Yuna, as Ren, bows too deeply.

RIKA smiles.

INT. NAGI SHRINE STAGE - NIGHT

Ren, inside Yuna's body, stands in shrine clothing before villagers.

Drums begin.

Hana whispers from the side.

HANA
Left hand first.

Ren raises the wrong hand.

The crowd murmurs.

Asa taps a drum once.

Ren watches Asa's fingers.

He follows.

The dance steadies.

On the final turn, he sees the comet hanging over the lake.

INT. REN'S ROOM - MORNING

Ren wakes.

He checks his phone.

Yuna has written:

YOUR JOB IS EASY. YOUR SENIOR IS BEAUTIFUL.

Ren shouts.

REN
No.

He scrolls.

There is a date marked on his calendar.

This Saturday.

INT. YUNA'S ROOM - MORNING

Yuna wakes.

She sees Ren's note:

YOUR GRANDMOTHER KNOWS SOMETHING.

Yuna looks toward the shrine courtyard.

Asa sweeps leaves below.

MONTAGE - BODY EXCHANGE DAYS

- Ren wakes as Yuna and studies village dialect from Hana.
- Yuna wakes as Ren and learns Tokyo train gates by following strangers.
- Ren saves Yuna from a teacher's public scolding by answering with exact shrine history.
- Yuna helps Ren talk to Rika without snapping.
- They argue through phone notes.
- They stop arguing and begin leaving useful maps.
- Ren draws Nagi lake from memory.
- Yuna draws Tokyo towers on her bedroom wall.
- Both wake laughing.

END MONTAGE.

EXT. NAGI RIDGE - SUNSET

Yuna, herself, walks with Asa toward an old crater shrine above the lake.

Sora carries a box of braided cords.

ASA
The old shrine is across the waterline.

YUNA
Why keep offerings where no one visits?

ASA
Because a promise is not for the person watching.

They cross a rope bridge.

INT. CRATER SHRINE - SUNSET

Inside the cave, hundreds of red cords hang from the ceiling.

Asa places a small sealed gourd on stone.

ASA
Our family leaves a thread for tomorrow.

Yuna touches one old cord.

For one second, she hears a Tokyo train.

EXT. TOKYO - WEEKEND - DAY

Ren, himself, waits outside a gallery.

Rika arrives.

REN
Thank you for coming.

Rika studies him.

RIKA
You sound like you rehearsed with someone.

Ren almost says yes.

He sees a red cord tied around his wrist.

He does not remember tying it.

INT. GALLERY - DAY

Ren and Rika walk through architectural models.

Ren talks too fast.

Rika stops at a photograph of a mountain lake.

RIKA
You drew this place, didn't you?

Ren sees the photo label:

NAGI BASIN, ARCHIVE IMAGE.

He steps closer.

The lake is the village from his dreams.

EXT. TOKYO PEDESTRIAN BRIDGE - SUNSET

Rika hands Ren a bandage.

RIKA
You're looking for someone.

REN
Maybe.

RIKA
Then don't take someone else on the search.

She leaves.

Ren looks at the red cord.

INT. REN'S ROOM - NIGHT

Ren calls Yuna.

No signal.

He sends messages.

Nothing.

He sleeps with his phone in his hand.

Morning.

Still himself.

Another morning.

Still himself.

He opens every note they wrote.

Yuna's messages begin disappearing.

He copies one sentence onto paper before it vanishes:

THE LAKE IS A BELL.

EXT. RURAL TRAIN - DAY

Ren, Kenta, and Mio travel into the mountains.

Ren holds sketches of Nagi's streets.

MIO
You dreamed a whole town?

REN
I lived there badly.

KENTA
That is different from dreaming?

Ren watches mountains pass.

EXT. SMALL STATION - LATE AFTERNOON

They show Ren's sketch to a station attendant.

The attendant points down the road.

ATTENDANT
Ask the noodle shop.

INT. NOODLE SHOP - EVENING

The cook studies Ren's drawing.

COOK
Old Nagi.

Ren sits forward.

COOK
Nobody calls it that now.

He points to a framed newspaper.

The headline shows the comet impact.

Ren stands.

The room sound drops away.

EXT. NAGI RUINS OVERLOOK - NIGHT

Ren looks over a massive lake.

No village.

Only water and memorial lights.

Mio checks her phone.

MIO
Impact was three years ago.

Ren shakes his head.

REN
I was there last week.

Kenta touches his shoulder.

Ren pulls away.

INT. LOCAL LIBRARY - NIGHT

Ren scrolls through disaster records.

Faces of the dead.

He finds Yuna's school photo.

He finds Hana.

He finds Daichi.

He finds Sora.

He does not find Asa.

His hand trembles.

The librarian turns off one row of lights.

LIBRARIAN
We're closing.

Ren writes YUNA HOSHINO on his palm.

The ink fades before it dries.

EXT. MOUNTAIN TRAIL - DAWN

Ren climbs alone toward the crater shrine.

Rain begins.

He slips.

The red cord on his wrist catches on a branch.

He sees the shrine cave across the waterline.

INT. CRATER SHRINE - DAY

Ren enters.

The hanging cords sway though there is no wind.

He finds the sealed gourd.

It bears Yuna's family crest.

He opens it.

Inside: fermented festival water and a braided cord.

Ren kneels.

REN
If this is a door, open.

He drinks.

The cave tilts.

The cords pull upward like falling rain in reverse.

CUT TO BLACK.

INT. YUNA'S ROOM - MORNING

Ren wakes in Yuna's body.

Sora opens the door.

SORA
Festival day.

Ren runs to the mirror.

Yuna's face stares back.

He laughs once.

Then remembers the newspaper.

REN
Today.

EXT. NAGI STREET - DAY

Ren, as Yuna, runs through town.

Children hang comet lanterns.

Election trucks pass.

Mayor Hoshino speaks from a platform.

MAYOR
Tonight, Nagi welcomes the comet.

Ren pushes through the crowd.

REN
You have to evacuate.

The mayor sees his daughter's face.

MAYOR
Not here.

INT. MAYOR'S OFFICE - DAY

Ren stands across from the mayor.

The mayor's aides hover.

REN
The fragment hits after sunset. The lake becomes a crater.

MAYOR
Who put you up to this?

REN
No one.

MAYOR
Then stop speaking like a stranger in my daughter's mouth.

Ren freezes.

The mayor knows something.

MAYOR
Your mother spoke that way before she died.

Ren has no answer.

EXT. SCHOOL GROUNDS - AFTERNOON

Ren finds Hana and Daichi.

HANA
You're pale.

REN
I need you to believe an impossible thing.

DAICHI
Again?

Ren points to the substation above town.

REN
We need everyone at the school before dark.

Hana looks at the festival stalls.

HANA
That's not a prank.

REN
No.

Daichi opens his tool bag.

DAICHI
Then we need a better lie.

INT. SCHOOL BROADCAST ROOM - AFTERNOON

Hana tests the microphone.

Feedback shrieks.

Daichi spreads a hand-drawn map.

Ren marks evacuation routes.

HANA
What if they arrest us?

REN
They can arrest us at the school.

Daichi looks at him.

DAICHI
You talk like you won't be here tomorrow.

Ren says nothing.

EXT. SUBSTATION - SUNSET

Daichi sets a timed smoke charge.

His hands shake.

DAICHI
My dad built this.

REN
Can you make it fail without hurting anyone?

Daichi nods.

Hana watches the comet brighten.

HANA
It is too pretty to be a warning.

EXT. CRATER SHRINE RIDGE - SUNSET

Yuna, in Ren's body, wakes on the ridge.

She stumbles out of the cave.

She sees Tokyo clothes on her body.

She sees Ren's name written on her wrist.

It fades.

Across the basin, Ren, in Yuna's body, runs up the ridge.

They cannot see each other.

The sun touches the horizon.

For one brief band of light, their bodies separate from time.

Ren stands as himself.

Yuna stands as herself.

They face each other.

Silence.

Then both laugh, breathless.

YUNA
You're shorter than your notes.

REN
You're worse at apologies than your notes.

They reach for each other.

Their hands meet.

The red cord drops between their wrists.

REN
Write your name.

Yuna gives him a marker.

REN
You first.

The light thins.

Ren writes on Yuna's palm.

Yuna writes on Ren's.

They do not look until the last second.

YUNA
If I forget?

REN
Run anyway.

The sun disappears.

Yuna is alone on the ridge.

She opens her palm.

The ink is smeared.

Only one stroke remains.

EXT. NAGI FESTIVAL - NIGHT

Yuna runs through lanterns.

The comet splits above town.

She grabs Hana.

YUNA
Broadcast now.

HANA
Where did you go?

YUNA
Forward.

INT. SCHOOL BROADCAST ROOM - NIGHT

Hana speaks into the microphone.

HANA
This is an emergency drill. All residents proceed to Nagi High School.

The school speaker dies.

Daichi slams the mixer.

DAICHI
Substation did not trip.

Yuna looks toward town hall.

YUNA
Then we use his voice.

INT. TOWN HALL - NIGHT

The mayor listens to aides argue.

Yuna bursts in.

YUNA
Mom knew, didn't she?

The room goes quiet.

MAYOR
Leave.

YUNA
She left cords for days she could not reach.

The mayor's mask cracks.

YUNA
I am asking as your daughter.

Outside, the first siren begins.

The mayor looks at the comet on the security monitor.

He picks up the emergency phone.

MAYOR
Open the school shelter.

EXT. NAGI - NIGHT

Town speakers come alive.

People look up from festival stalls.

Firefighters wave traffic uphill.

Children carry lanterns toward the school.

Daichi pulls elders into a truck.

Hana runs with the broadcast handset.

Yuna stands in the street.

The comet fragment tears open the sky.

EXT. SCHOOL HILL - NIGHT

Yuna reaches the hill as the impact flash lights her face.

The town below disappears in white.

Sound drops out.

Only the red cord in her hand moves in the blast wind.

CUT TO BLACK.

INT. TOKYO APARTMENT - MORNING

Ren wakes.

He gasps.

His palm is blank.

He grabs paper.

He writes:

Y...

The rest will not come.

On his wrist is the red cord.

EXT. NAGI MEMORIAL PARK - YEARS LATER - DAY

Ren, twenty-five, stands before a new town map.

The old crater lake is marked as preserved water.

A plaque lists evacuation survivors.

He reads names.

He does not know what he is looking for.

INT. TOKYO ARCHITECTURE OFFICE - DAY

Ren works on a community shelter model.

His coworker places coffee beside him.

COWORKER
You design every roof like it is waiting for rain.

Ren looks at the model's red evacuation lines.

REN
Maybe every roof is.

EXT. TOKYO STREET - WINTER EVENING

Yuna, twenty-eight, exits a planning office with rolled maps.

She wears a red cord around her bag handle.

She stops before a poster:

REGIONAL DISASTER MEMORY EXHIBITION.

Ren walks past behind her.

Neither turns.

The cord swings.

INT. SUBWAY - MORNING

Ren stands in a crowded train.

Across the passing train, Yuna stands at another door.

They see each other for one second.

Both turn.

The trains separate.

EXT. TOKYO STREETS - CONTINUOUS

Ren runs out of the station.

Yuna runs down another stairway.

They search through crowds.

At an intersection, they miss each other by a bus length.

Rain begins.

Ren stops.

He touches the red cord.

Yuna does the same.

EXT. SHRINE STAIRS - LATE AFTERNOON

Ren climbs from the lower street.

Yuna descends from the upper street.

They pass.

Both stop.

Neither turns first.

Ren breathes out.

Yuna closes her umbrella.

They turn.

REN
I know this sounds strange.

YUNA
It does.

Ren laughs.

Yuna almost does.

REN
Have we met?

Yuna looks at the red cord on his wrist.

She opens her bag.

The matching cord hangs there.

YUNA
I don't know.

She steps down one stair.

YUNA
But I have been looking.

Ren steps up one stair.

REN
Me too.

They stand in the rain.

Yuna smiles.

YUNA
Then before we forget again...

Ren nods.

REN
Your name?

Yuna opens her mouth.

FADE OUT.
```

## Draft Notes

- This draft preserves the functional architecture of the public synopsis: dream/body exchange, crossed rural/city lives, comet threat, failed memory, disaster prevention, adult search.
- It does not yet have the full density, comedy, music-driven montage rhythm, or micro-scene texture of a 132-page screenplay.
- It should be treated as v1 for comparison and revision, not as the final quality target.
