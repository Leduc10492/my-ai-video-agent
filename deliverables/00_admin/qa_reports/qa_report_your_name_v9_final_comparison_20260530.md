## Why It Still Falls Short

1. **Scene count and transition rhythm are still low.**  
   v9 has about 65 slugline/transition candidates versus the PDF's roughly 164. Even allowing for extraction noise, this is a large gap.

2. **Many v9 scenes carry too many functions.**  
   A single v9 scene often handles setup, reversal, relationship, and plot mechanics at once. The comparison PDF spreads those functions across smaller beats.

3. **Dialogue accumulation is still thin.**  
   v9 has enough cues to be readable, but not enough repeated conversational pressure to build the same level of relationship texture.

4. **The date/misdirection and adult aftermath are now present but still short.**  
   They function correctly but do not yet have the emotional room of a full feature screenplay.

5. **The rescue sequence is operational but still compressed.**  
   It has the right blockers, but the crowd, school, officials, and family response need more beat-by-beat escalation.

## Required v10 Direction

Do not change the premise. Expand v9 into a higher scene-count screenplay.

| Target | Required change |
| --- | --- |
| Scene density | Increase from about 65 to at least 95-110 slugline/transition units before claiming parity. |
| Body-swap middle | Add smaller school/home/work scenes with clear single-value turns. |

| Date/misdirection | Split gallery/cafe/bridge into fuller separate scenes with a sharper emotional reversal. |
