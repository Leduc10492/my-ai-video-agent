## Executive Finding

`01_script_delayed-blue_v7.md` is structurally playable and more natural than v6, but the source comparison changes the gate result if the target is source-quality parity. v7 should no longer be treated as `hold_current` for the Hoshi no Koe exercise. It should be treated as `revise_structure` with one primary problem:

> v7 turns the premise into a repair/public-broadcast action chain, while the source builds emotional force from measurable message delay, asymmetric aging, and ordinary sensory memory.

This does not mean v7 is useless. It means v7 solved the wrong problem too well.

## Main Differences

### 1. v7 is too repairable

The source's core object is ordinary communication: phone/email. The pain comes from physics and time, not from broken hardware. v7 replaces that with a blue radio and repair ecosystem. That gives Lin agency, but it also makes the central wound feel technically negotiable.

Revision implication: keep the blue radio only as a local-world anchor. Do not let repair skill repeatedly solve or redirect the central emotional problem.

### 2. v7 has external plot pressure where the source has time pressure

The source's biggest turns are delay jumps: the message will arrive in one year, then the relationship shifts toward an eight-year scale. v7's biggest turns are typhoon rescue, public broadcast, transmitter overload, and locator sacrifice. These are playable, but they pull the genre toward disaster rescue.

Revision implication: every major set piece must change when a message arrives, how old it is, or what age/version of the sender it represents.

### 3. v7's Earth side is morally active, but less emotionally trapped

Lin choosing to fix a public broadcast is dramatically clean, but it gives him a heroic moral choice. The source's Earth-side pain is quieter: school, season changes, waiting, then deciding to stop waiting. That passivity is not a flaw; it is the emotional design.

Revision implication: Lin can still have life and work, but his key choices should be about whether to keep orienting his life around an impossible delayed signal, not whether to save a neighborhood.

### 4. v7 has fewer message/voice fragments

The source transcript carries much of the story through emails, VO, and delayed fragments. v7 has fuller scenes and more visible behavior, but fewer message units. That is why v7 reads more like a conventional screenplay and less like the source's broken-distance form.

Revision implication: increase short message fragments, timestamped arrivals, failed sends, old recordings, and wrong-age replies. The issue is not total page count; it is communication density.

### 5. v7's dialogue is more human than v6, but still too designed

v7 improved the AI-like short lines, but many exchanges still perform craft neatness: joke, repair metaphor, callback. The source's dialogue is often plain, incomplete, adolescent, and carried by context rather than wit.

Revision implication: reduce clever banter by 30-40%. Let more lines be ordinary, awkward, unfinished, or informational.

### 6. v7 underuses sensory memory

The source's late emotional peak leans on ordinary sensations and places rather than plot facts. v7 has sea, ice shop, local weather, shop sounds, and tape, but they often remain tied to mechanism and motif. They need to become memory texture.

Revision implication: late draft should include a concentrated sensory-memory passage, but it must be original to v7's world: sea wall at low tide, school broadcast lag, rust smell, repair-shop solder, wet stairs, cheap ice, typhoon mud.

### 7. v7's ending over-demonstrates

The source ending is minimal: distance remains, but shared existence is confirmed. v7's final send has strong action staging and a clean verbal payoff. It works as a climax, but it explains too much and wins too clearly.

Revision implication: keep the one-way send, but make the result smaller, later, and less confirmable. The audience should feel the cost and the uncertainty, not just the successful transmission.

## Recommended Next Gate

Decision: `revise_structure`

Reason: the comparison reveals a P1 parity issue, not a line-polish issue. v7 is coherent as an original short, but the exercise goal is now to approach the source's quality logic. The next version should not merely polish dialogue.

## v8 Rewrite Target

One primary problem: rebuild the delayed-communication engine.

Keep:

- original characters Lin / Xu
- blue radio and sea tape as original motifs
- no reunion
- 22-24 minute runtime
- copyright distance from source names, ship terms, enemies, and exact scenes

Change:

- reduce typhoon/public-broadcast weight
- add explicit delay stages: minutes/hours -> months -> years -> wrong-age message
- make each communication event change the relationship state
- increase message/VO fragments
- make Earth-side passage of time visible through ordinary life, not only repair jobs
- make far-side conflict simpler: mission duty versus one private sentence
- make final confirmation smaller and less technically victorious

## If Preserving v7 As Original

If the goal changes from source-parity exercise to an original short inspired by distant communication, v7 can remain `hold_current`. It has a clear object chain, playable rescue/action beats, and production-friendly locations.

If the goal remains source-quality comparison, v7 should be archived as a useful wrong-branch draft and followed by v8.
