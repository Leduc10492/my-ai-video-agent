## Executive Judgment

v6 is not yet at the same level as the comparison PDF. It has the right macro machinery but lacks the detailed scene-by-scene pressure that makes a feature screenplay feel executable.

The main issue is not the premise. The issue is density: the PDF sustains body-swap comedy, daily-life texture, supporting characters, musical/montage rhythm, and disaster mechanics across 132 pages. v6 compresses those functions into a readable but underbuilt draft.

## Why v6 Is Worse

1. **It confuses structure with screenplay.**  
   v6 has the correct sequence of functions, but many functions are summarized in one scene or montage. A produced feature needs scenes that let behavior accumulate.

2. **Body-swap comedy is underwritten.**  
   The comparison PDF has many school, home, work, friend, and gender-performance consequences. v6 has enough to explain the premise but not enough to create audience pleasure.

3. **Supporting characters are too instrumental.**  
   Hana, Daichi, Rika, Asa, and the mayor are mostly levers. The reference structure gives side characters recurring comic, social, logistical, and emotional pressure.

4. **The ritual/time system is not specific enough.**  
   Red cord and crater shrine are usable motifs, but the rules need physical actions, prohibitions, family history, and visual repetition.

5. **The midpoint reveal is too easy.**  
   In v6, the noodle shop/library path solves the town mystery quickly. The comparison PDF spreads the search across travel, sketches, memory, disbelief, and local proof.

6. **The evacuation lacks procedural credibility.**  
   v6 has a plan, broadcast room, mayor, and siren, but not enough complications: parents, police, school staff, transport, false alarms, panic, and time pressure.

7. **The ending is emotionally correct but too short.**  
   The final stairs recognition is in the right functional family, but v6 needs more adult-life loneliness and near-misses before that moment.
