# Checklists

## Story diagnostic

- What is the primary value at stake? How does it shift?
- What is the protagonist's conscious desire?
- What conflicts oppose that desire (internal, personal, societal, environmental)?
- Do major choices force a real cost (gain one value, lose another)?
- What events cause irreversible change rather than coincidence?
- How does the climax resolve the core value conflict?
- Is the inciting incident on-screen and early enough?
- Is there a clear crisis (mandatory scene) that tests the strongest opposing force?
- Does the story spine read as: inciting incident → complications → crisis → climax → resolution?
- Is there a clear causal link from inciting incident to climax?
- Are tension/relief waves building toward higher peaks?

## Scene diagnostic

- What is the key value in this scene (life/death, freedom/slavery, etc.)?
- What is the value at the start? At the end?
- What is the turning point that causes the change?
- What conflict produces that turning point?
- If the scene only explains information, can it be replaced or redesigned to turn a value?

## Sequence diagnostic

- Does each scene intensify the conflict?
- Do 2–5 scenes build toward a sequence peak?
- Is the sequence peak meaningful for the protagonist's desire and the story value arc?

## Character diagnostic

- Is the protagonist's desire explicit and active?
- Do they make decisions that create consequences?
- What is revealed about their inner nature over time?
- How does the character change by the end?
- Are antagonistic forces layered enough to force hard choices?

## Theme diagnostic

- What opposing values are repeatedly tested (justice/injustice, love/hate, etc.)?
- How does the story argue for one side through events?
- What final meaning is asserted at the climax?

## Exposition diagnostic

- Does the audience learn information only when they need and want it?
- Are reveals dramatized as actions or turning points rather than pure explanation?
- Are flashbacks treated as mini-stories with their own turning points?

## Audience-knowledge diagnostic

- Is the sequence operating as mystery, suspense, or dramatic irony?
- Is the mix intentional (e.g., suspense overall with pockets of mystery)?
