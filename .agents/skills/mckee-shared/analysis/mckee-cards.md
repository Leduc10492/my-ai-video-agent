# McKee Principle Cards

Format:
- ID
- Principle
- Use
- Audit question
- Common failure
- Fix moves
- Source anchor

MC-01
Principle: Story is guided by principles, not rigid formulas.
Use: Avoid template-only fixes.
Audit question: Are we forcing a model instead of serving the story?
Common failure: Formula-driven beats without organic causality.
Fix moves: Re-derive turning points from character desire and conflict.
Source anchor: Introduction

MC-02
Principle: Story issues outrank prose issues.
Use: Prioritize structure in review.
Audit question: Are we fixing dialogue while the structure is broken?
Common failure: Polishing scenes that should be cut or moved.
Fix moves: Diagnose story spine before line edits.
Source anchor: Introduction

MC-03
Principle: Value change drives story.
Use: Evaluate acts, sequences, and scenes.
Audit question: What value shifts from positive to negative (or vice versa)?
Common failure: Scenes with no meaningful change.
Fix moves: Add pressure and choice to force a turn.
Source anchor: Scene Design

MC-04
Principle: Beat is an action/reaction exchange.
Use: Micro-level scene crafting.
Audit question: Do beats alternate action and reaction with new info?
Common failure: Static exchanges or repeated beats.
Fix moves: Introduce a new tactic or revelation each beat.
Source anchor: Scene Design

MC-05
Principle: Scene is conflict that turns value.
Use: Scene diagnosis.
Audit question: Does the scene end in a different value state than it began?
Common failure: Expository scenes without conflict.
Fix moves: Tie exposition to a conflict and force a choice.
Source anchor: Scene Design

MC-06
Principle: Sequence is a series of scenes with a larger turn.
Use: Mid-level structure.
Audit question: Do the scenes build to a sequence-level reversal?
Common failure: Flat sequences that wander.
Fix moves: Define the sequence goal and its turning outcome.
Source anchor: Act Design

MC-07
Principle: Act ends in a major reversal.
Use: Act structure.
Audit question: Does each act end with a decisive value shift?
Common failure: Act breaks that feel arbitrary.
Fix moves: Rebuild act turns around a pivotal choice or event.
Source anchor: Act Design

MC-08
Principle: Inciting incident upsets balance and triggers desire.
Use: Story entry point.
Audit question: What event forces the protagonist into pursuit?
Common failure: A premise without a triggering disruption.
Fix moves: Add a clear event that creates a new objective.
Source anchor: The Inciting Incident

MC-09
Principle: Inciting incident timing must allow audience comprehension.
Use: Placement decisions.
Audit question: Do we understand enough to react to the incident?
Common failure: Too early to care or too late to engage.
Fix moves: Adjust setup or move the incident earlier.
Source anchor: The Inciting Incident

MC-10
Principle: Desire drives action; stakes define urgency.
Use: Character motivation.
Audit question: What does the protagonist want, and what is at risk?
Common failure: Vague or shifting objectives.
Fix moves: Make desire explicit and raise the cost of failure.
Source anchor: Structure and Character

MC-11
Principle: Obligatory scene pays off the inciting incident.
Use: Audience expectations.
Audit question: What must happen because of the story's setup?
Common failure: Missing or weak payoff.
Fix moves: Design a scene that forces the promised test.
Source anchor: Crisis, Climax, Resolution

MC-12
Principle: Crisis forces a choice between irreconcilable options.
Use: Pre-climax pressure.
Audit question: Is the protagonist forced to choose, not drift?
Common failure: Crisis solved by coincidence or luck.
Fix moves: Make the choice costly and character-revealing.
Source anchor: Crisis, Climax, Resolution

MC-13
Principle: Climax is the most intense, irreversible turn.
Use: Endgame design.
Audit question: Does the climax permanently change the story world?
Common failure: Soft endings or reversible outcomes.
Fix moves: Increase stakes and lock in consequences.
Source anchor: Crisis, Climax, Resolution

MC-14
Principle: Resolution confirms the new value state.
Use: Closing.
Audit question: Is the ending state clear and earned?
Common failure: Abrupt stop without payoff.
Fix moves: Show the new equilibrium in action.
Source anchor: Crisis, Climax, Resolution

MC-15
Principle: Causality beats coincidence.
Use: Plot logic.
Audit question: Do actions cause effects that cause new actions?
Common failure: Convenient saves or random twists.
Fix moves: Seed cause earlier or make choices trigger outcomes.
Source anchor: The Structure Spectrum

MC-16
Principle: Exposition should be motivated by conflict.
Use: Information delivery.
Audit question: Does the need to win/avoid loss drive the info?
Common failure: Info dumps or on-the-nose setup.
Fix moves: Hide facts in tactical dialogue or action.
Source anchor: Exposition

MC-17
Principle: Antagonism must be credible and escalating.
Use: Opposition design.
Audit question: Is the opposing force strong enough to win?
Common failure: Easy obstacles.
Fix moves: Add power, intelligence, or moral leverage to the opponent.
Source anchor: The Principle of Antagonism

MC-18
Principle: Active protagonists pursue desire; passive ones must face inner conflict.
Use: Protagonist choice.
Audit question: Is the protagonist driving or merely observing?
Common failure: Passive hero without inner struggle.
Fix moves: Add a decisive pursuit or deepen internal conflict.
Source anchor: The Structure Spectrum

MC-19
Principle: Choose a story design (archplot, miniplot, antiplot) deliberately.
Use: Structural intent.
Audit question: Does the design match the story's aims?
Common failure: Mixed signals about reality and causality.
Fix moves: Align tone, time, and causality rules with the chosen design.
Source anchor: The Structure Spectrum

MC-20
Principle: Reality rules must be consistent.
Use: Worldbuilding logic.
Audit question: Are the story's rules broken for convenience?
Common failure: One-off miracles or rule breaks.
Fix moves: Foreshadow exceptions or redesign the rule.
Source anchor: The Structure Spectrum

MC-21
Principle: Progression requires escalation or complication.
Use: Pacing.
Audit question: Does each scene increase pressure or change direction?
Common failure: Repeated conflicts with no escalation.
Fix moves: Increase stakes, add a new constraint, or reverse fortune.
Source anchor: Scene Analysis

MC-22
Principle: Gap between expectation and result creates turning points.
Use: Surprise and reversal.
Audit question: Are outcomes different from what is expected?
Common failure: Predictable outcomes.
Fix moves: Redesign tactics to produce an unexpected but causal result.
Source anchor: Scene Analysis

MC-23
Principle: Subtext emerges from conflict and desire, not explanation.
Use: Dialogue.
Audit question: Are characters saying what they want directly without pressure?
Common failure: On-the-nose lines.
Fix moves: Give characters competing objectives and let subtext surface.
Source anchor: The Text

MC-24
Principle: Character is revealed by choice under pressure.
Use: Characterization.
Audit question: Do we see decisions that expose values?
Common failure: Description without decisive action.
Fix moves: Force a choice with a cost.
Source anchor: Character

MC-25
Principle: Enter scenes late, exit early.
Use: Tighten pacing.
Audit question: Can the scene start later and end sooner?
Common failure: Long lead-ins and cool-downs.
Fix moves: Cut preamble and end on the turn.
Source anchor: Scene Design

MC-26
Principle: Stakes must be clear and rising.
Use: Momentum.
Audit question: What is at risk now, and how is it worse than before?
Common failure: Flat stakes.
Fix moves: Clarify consequences and increase cost.
Source anchor: Act Design

MC-27
Principle: Variation beats repetition.
Use: Avoid monotony.
Audit question: Are we repeating the same conflict outcome?
Common failure: Multiple scenes with the same beat.
Fix moves: Change tactic, setting, or power balance.
Source anchor: Problems and Solutions

MC-28
Principle: Rewrite is analysis plus decisive re-organization.
Use: Draft iteration.
Audit question: Did we diagnose the cause, not just the symptom?
Common failure: Patch fixes without structural change.
Fix moves: Re-outline before rewriting.
Source anchor: A Writer's Method
