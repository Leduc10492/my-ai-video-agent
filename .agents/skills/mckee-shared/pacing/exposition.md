# Exposition and backstory

## Exposition

- Reveal only when the audience needs and wants the information.
- Avoid dialogue that tells a character what they already know.
- Prefer dramatized revelation over plain explanation.

## Backstory reveals

- Use backstory to create turning points at moments of crisis.
- A reveal is strongest when it changes the current value state.

## Flashbacks

- Treat flashbacks as mini-stories with their own inciting incident and turning point.
- A flat or purely descriptive flashback slows momentum.

## Dream and montage

- Dream sequences and montage often dilute dramatized exposition.
- Use sparingly; they are easy to overuse as shortcuts.

## Voiceover

- If the story works without voiceover, cut it.
- Use voiceover only for counterpoint or meaning that cannot be dramatized.
