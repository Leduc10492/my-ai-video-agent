# Mystery, suspense, dramatic irony

## Audience knowledge modes

- Mystery: audience knows less than the character; curiosity about facts.
- Suspense: audience knows the same; curiosity about outcome.
- Dramatic irony: audience knows more; curiosity about how/why characters act.

## Mixing modes

- Most stories mix modes; suspense is a common base with pockets of mystery or irony.
- Shifts should be intentional and track the story's value arc.

## Mystery variants

- Closed mystery: the crime is off-screen; audience asks "who did it?"
- Open mystery: the crime is witnessed; audience asks "how will they be caught?"

## Suspense outcomes

- Suspense can resolve upward, downward, or with irony.
