# Story spine

## Five-part design

- Inciting incident
- Progressive complications
- Crisis (mandatory scene)
- Climax
- Resolution

## Inciting incident

- A dynamic event that breaks equilibrium and flips a value.
- Must occur on-screen for the main plot; subplots can be off-screen.
- Should be early (roughly the first quarter of the story; ~30 minutes in a feature).
- Launches the protagonist onto the pursuit path.

## Mandatory crisis scene

- The inciting incident projects a crisis the audience expects to see.
- This scene pits the protagonist against the strongest opposing force.
- Deliver it; do not evade or replace it.

## Climax and resolution

- Climax is a deep value change, not just spectacle.
- Resolution closes the value arc established by the inciting incident.
- Avoid coincidence or deus ex machina at the ending.

## Causal lock

- The inciting incident should make the climax inevitable.
- If you cannot say "because of the inciting incident, the climax must happen," the spine is weak.

## Open vs closed endings

- A triangle choice (A vs B vs C) can yield a closed ending by forcing a real sacrifice.
- If the story only oscillates between yes/no without a decisive choice, the ending often feels open.
